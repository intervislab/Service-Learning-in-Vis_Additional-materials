SERVICE-LEARNING IN DATA VISUALIZATION

Service-Learning is a credited pedagogical model in which classroom learning objectives are aligned with community service to meet both educational and community goals. It not only helps students learn data visualization better but also helps the community by aiding non-profit organizations with their data needs.

Most of these non-profit organizations lack the time and expertise it demands to probe into their ever growing data and get insights from them and this in turn hinders them from making valuable decisions for increasing the impact of their organization. It also makes it very difficult for them to communicate their impact to potential funders in order to retain sustainability. On the hand, data visualization students learn the skills of data analysis, visual encoding techniques, evaluation, task analysis etc. in class but do not have a way to implement their skills in a real-world setting with real clients. Hence, Service-Learning makes a way to bring these two parties together, creating a perfect synergy where the organizations can gain insight from their data through analysis and visualization at no cost, while the students get to implement their classroom skills to solve real world problems.

Through the integration of Service-Learning with visualization pedagogy, the students are able to more effectively learn the concepts and skills taught in the classroom, develop professional and interpersonal skills unattainable in a classroom setting, and experience first-hand how their visualization skills can lead to a positive impact on their community.
